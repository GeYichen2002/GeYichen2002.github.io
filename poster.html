Seasonal Health Guide | Traditional Wisdom and Modern Science

This is a series of WeChat posters and text tweets created for institutions such as People's Daily Online and the National Healthcare Security Administration. Taking the 24 solar terms as the thread, it integrates the wisdom of traditional Chinese medicine health preservation and evidence - based medical knowledge. Using visual design to create the "health code in time", it breathes contemporary vitality into traditional culture.

The 24 Solar Terms (二十四节气), rooted in ancient Chinese agricultural practices, are a unique timekeeping system dividing the year into 24 periods based on the sun's position. These terms reflect seasonal changes, guiding farming, festivals, and daily life. More importantly, they embody the philosophy of living in harmony with nature, which is deeply tied to traditional Chinese health practices.

🌱Spring (Start of Spring to Grain Rain)

Spring symbolizes renewal, as nature awakens from winter. Traditional health practices focus on detoxifying the liver and boosting immunity with fresh greens (e.g., spinach, sprouts) and light exercises like tai chi. Avoid stagnant energy by staying active and managing stress, as spring is linked to the liver in Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM).

☀️Summer (Start of Summer to Major Heat)

Summer's intense heat calls for protecting the heart and staying hydrated. Cooling foods like watermelon, mung beans, and herbal teas (e.g., chrysanthemum) help combat inflammation and heatstroke. TCM advises avoiding excessive cold drinks to prevent "dampness," which can weaken digestion, and embracing mindfulness to calm the mind amid rising temperatures.

🍂Autumn (Start of Autumn to Frost's Descent)

Autumn's dry, cooling air requires nourishing the lungs with moistening foods like pears, honey, and almonds. TCM emphasizes balancing yin energy to ward off seasonal dryness and respiratory issues. Gradually shift to warming soups and spices (ginger, cinnamon) to prepare the body for winter while maintaining emotional equilibrium.

❄️Winter (Start of Winter to Major Cold)

Winter is a time to conserve energy and strengthen the kidneys, according to TCM. Hearty stews, bone broth, and black foods (black beans, sesame) help build resilience against cold. Prioritize rest, warm clothing, and gentle indoor exercises like yoga to support circulation and immunity, while avoiding overexertion to align with nature's dormant rhythm.

By aligning lifestyle with the 24 Solar Terms, individuals can enhance physical resilience, emotional stability, and long-term wellness—a timeless wisdom for modern health! 🌱🍵

夏至 - Summer Solstice

夏至是一年中白昼最长的一天，阳气达到顶峰。此时应注意防暑降温，保持充足的休息，饮食宜清淡，多食用苦味食物以清热。

The Summer Solstice is the longest day of the year, when yang energy reaches its peak. During this time, it's important to stay cool, get adequate rest, maintain a light diet, and consume more bitter foods to clear heat.

大暑 - Great Heat

大暑是一年中最热的时期，此时应特别注意防暑防晒，多喝水，避免在正午外出。饮食上可多食用西瓜、绿豆等清热解暑的食物。

Great Heat is the hottest period of the year. During this time, it's especially important to protect against heat and sun exposure, drink plenty of water, and avoid going out at noon. Dietary recommendations include watermelon, mung beans, and other cooling foods.

立秋 - Beginning of Autumn

立秋标志着秋季的开始，阳气开始收敛，阴气渐长。此时应注意保养肺部，防止秋燥伤肺。饮食上宜滋阴润燥，可多食用梨、百合等食物。

The Beginning of Autumn marks the start of fall, when yang energy begins to recede and yin energy gradually increases. During this time, it's important to nourish the lungs and prevent autumn dryness from harming them. The diet should focus on moistening dryness, with foods like pears and lilies.

处暑 - End of Heat

处暑意味着炎热的夏季即将结束，但昼热夜凉的温差较大。此时应注意适时增减衣物，防止感冒。饮食上可选择温性平和的食物，如山药、莲子等。

The End of Heat signifies that the hot summer is coming to an end, but there can be significant temperature differences between day and night. During this time, it's important to adjust clothing accordingly to prevent colds. The diet should include mild, warming foods such as yams and lotus seeds.

秋分 - Autumnal Equinox

秋分是昼夜平分的时节，阴阳平衡。此时应注意养阴潜阳，保持情绪平和。饮食上宜滋阴润肺，可多食用银耳、蜂蜜等食物。

The Autumnal Equinox is when day and night are equal in length, representing a balance of yin and yang. During this time, it's important to nourish yin and subdue yang, maintaining emotional balance. The diet should focus on nourishing yin and moistening the lungs, with foods like white fungus and honey.

谷雨 - Grain Rain

谷雨是春季最后一个节气，雨水增多，有利于谷物生长。此时阳气上升，万物生长旺盛。饮食上宜清淡，可多食用春笋、菠菜等时令蔬菜。

Grain Rain is the last solar term of spring, with increased rainfall beneficial for grain growth. During this time, yang energy rises and all things grow vigorously. The diet should be light, with seasonal vegetables like bamboo shoots and spinach.

立春 - Beginning of Spring

立春标志着春季的开始，万物复苏，阳气开始生发。此时应顺应春季阳气升发的特点，保持心情舒畅，饮食宜温补而不宜过于寒凉。

The Beginning of Spring marks the start of spring, when all things revive and yang energy begins to grow. During this time, it's important to follow the characteristics of rising yang energy in spring, maintain a good mood, and have a warm diet rather than too cold foods.

冬至 - Winter Solstice

冬至是一年中白昼最短的一天，阳气最弱，阴气最盛。此时应注意保暖，早睡晚起，顺应自然。饮食上宜温补，可食用羊肉、栗子等温热食物。

The Winter Solstice is the shortest day of the year, when yang energy is at its weakest and yin energy is at its strongest. During this time, it's important to stay warm, go to bed early and rise late, following the rhythm of nature. The diet should focus on warming and nourishing foods such as mutton and chestnuts.
